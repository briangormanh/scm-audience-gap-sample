02

The four openings

Not "start a blog" — they already have one. These are the high-demand topics the existing blog skips, ranked by the gap between what shoppers want and what's published.

Opening 01 · biggest gap

A "what's wrong with my plant?" library

Leaf & Loam has dozens of styling posts and not one that answers its audience's #1 search. Yellow leaves is the most-wanted, least-served topic by a mile.

An article we'd write
"Yellow Leaves? Here's Exactly What Your Plant Is Telling You"
A simple visual guide: the symptom, the likely cause (too much water, too little light…), and the fix. Each fix links the watering can, the moisture meter, or the right plant from the shop.
Why it wins  "leaves turning yellow" 9,900/mo + "drooping" 3,600/mo. A worried owner who finds the fix here buys their next plant here too.
Opening 02 · most-asked how-to

Plain-English watering guides

They've covered plant trends six different ways. They've never answered the question every new owner asks first: how often do I water this?

An article we'd write
"How Often to Actually Water Every Popular Houseplant"
One simple chart: plant, how often, how to tell when it's thirsty. Filmed once as a video, written up as the post, sliced into a week of Reels.
Why it wins  "how often to water a snake plant" 6,600/mo + "overwatered plant" 4,400/mo. Overwatering is the #1 way people kill plants — and the #1 reason they stop buying.
Opening 03 · reframe what exists

Buyer guides by situation, not by style

They have roundups — but aimed at aesthetics ("plants for a boho shelf"). The audience searches by their problem ("low light," "hard to kill"), and that's where the buyers are.

An article we'd write
"11 Houseplants That Survive a Dark Apartment"
A friendly roundup that solves "low light" anxiety, each plant linking straight to its shop page. Same format they're good at, pointed at real demand.
Why it wins  "best low-light indoor plants" 8,100/mo + "plants for beginners" 1,900/mo — far bigger than any styling term they rank for.
Opening 04 · turns a fear into a sale

A pet-safe plant guide

"Will this hurt my cat?" stops a lot of purchases cold. The blog has nothing on it. Answer it once and you remove the biggest objection pet owners have.

An article we'd write
"Which of Our Plants Are Safe for Cats and Dogs?"
A clear safe / not-safe list that doubles as a filtered "pet-safe" shop collection — turning a worry into a curated set of things to buy.
Why it wins  "are houseplants safe for cats" 2,400/mo. High anxiety, high intent, and it unlocks every pet owner who was afraid to buy.
